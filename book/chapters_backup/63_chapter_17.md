a switch. It’s calibration. And when your nervous system was wired in a glass box,
calibrated by violence, reinforced by abandonment, and trained by shame,
calibration does not come naturally. It has to be learned. Painfully. Slowly. With
repetition. With failure. With repair. For years, I treated relationships like threat
assessments. That is the truth. Not romance. Not partnership. Not safety. Threat
assessment. Who is mad? Who is leaving? Who is lying? Who is about to turn cold?
What did that tone mean? Why did that message change? Why are they quiet? What
did I do wrong? What am I about to lose? That is not love. That is surveillance. But
when your body learned early that people can become dangerous

Boundaries Are Not Walls Repair starts with boundaries. Not walls. Boundaries. I
had walls. Plenty of them. Isolation. Silence. Avoidance. Numbing. Disappearing.
Those were walls. Walls keep everything out. They protect you, but they also trap
you. Boundaries are different. Boundaries define where I end and someone else
begins. They say: This is what I can hold. This is what I can’t. This is what I will
discuss. This is what I won’t be called. This is where I stay. This is where I step back.
This is what love can ask of me. This is what love

is a location. It tells the truth about where you are. And if a relationship cannot
survive you having a location, it was not connection. It was control. That is a hard

someone might leave. So boundaries feel like standing on a cliff. But without them,
you do not become loving. You become absorbent. You soak up everything until you
